Pillar 7 - Security & Privacy

7.1 - Government Regulations

Does the website comply with federal privacy/security regulations both domestically and internationally?

NIST 800-53 (Appendix J)?

Yes No

If unsure, please refer to NIST 800-53 checklist

NIST 800-53 (Appendix J), "Security and Privacy Controls for Federal Information Systems and Organizations," provides a catalog of security controls for all U.S. federal information systems except those related to national security.

NIST 800-122?

Yes No

If unsure, please refer to NIST 800-122 guidelines

NIST 800-122, "Guide to Protecting the Confidentiality of Personally Identifiable Information," provides agencies with guidelines for a risk-based approach to protecting the confidentiality of personally identifiable information (PII).

Privacy Act of 1974?

Yes No

If unsure, please refer to Privacy Act of 1974

The Privacy Act of 1974 establishes a code of fair information practices that governs the collection, maintenance, use, and dissemination of information about individuals that is maintained in systems of records by federal agencies.

Paperwork Reduction Act (1995)?

Yes No

If unsure, please refer to Paperwork Reduction Act

The Paperwork Reduction Act (1995) requires that agencies obtain Office of Management and Budget (OMB) approval before requesting most types of information from the public.

E-Government Act of 2002?

Yes No

If unsure, please refer to E-Government Act of 2002

The E-Government Act of 2002 is a statute whose purpose is to improve the management and promotion of electronic government services and processes by establishing a framework of measures that require using Internet-based information technology to improve citizen access to government information and services.

Office of Management & Budget (OMB)?

Yes No

If unsure, please refer to OMB

The OMB details multiple guidelines regarding privacy and inter-agency sharing of personal data.

Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA)?

Yes No

If unsure, please refer to COPPA

COPPA is a law created to protect the privacy of children under 13.
